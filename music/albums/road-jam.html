All songs written by Kevin Ballantyne except where noted

With a gap of about 9 years since my last album, Road Jam reflects events and ideas from nearly the last decade. As usual, most, if not all, songs on this album originate from personal experiences.

‘Let's Rock’ is based on a jam with some friends. We played until the neighbours hit the walls.

Both ‘Aninaatig’ and ‘Jet Off Into The Night’ are based on one place, my aunt and uncle's farm near Perth, Ontario. The farm was named Aninaatig meaning 'maple tree' in Ojibwe. The former song is based on my childhood memories of the farm. Although 'Jet Off Into The Night' was something a friend of mine once said, the words are based on the many times we bid farewell to my uncle as we left the farm at night.

‘Running To Nowhere’ I wrote about the passing of a good friend of mine in 2021. This album is dedicated to his memory.

‘The Rocketship Is Gone’ is about some of my memories of my grandparents' house in Elmvale Acres, a community it Ottawa. I wrote the chorus while I was driving around the neighbourhood. As a child, I always remembered a lighthouse, rocketship and train outside the Museum of Science & Technology. However, on this drive I noticed the rocketship was gone. I was relieved to see the lighthouse was still there as a reminder of my childhood. While somethings may change, other things can stay the same.

There are a few songs that I wrote based on some of my travels. The title of ‘All Roads Lead To Salida’ came when I was in Chile and my cousin and I saw all these signs pointing to 'salida'. My cousin said "I guess all roads lead to salida" without us knowing that 'salida' means exit. The chorus to ‘Let The Sun Shine On Me’ I wrote while on a high-speed train in Japan. ‘The Western Pines’ is about my experience when going for a short hike at Whistler in British Columbia.

‘One More For The Road’ is about looking for new opportunities in life as I wrote it around the time I was looking for work.
